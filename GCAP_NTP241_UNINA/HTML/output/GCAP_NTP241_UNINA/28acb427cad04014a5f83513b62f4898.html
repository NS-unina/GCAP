(C-03) Autopilot

ControlEntity

GCAP_NTP241_UNINA > GCAP_NTP241_UNINA > STPA Analysis > Control Structure > (C-03) Autopilot

No description.

Content

  • The Autopilot shall not generate motor control commands unless all sensor data (IMU, GPS, Compass) are valid and consistent.
  • The Autopilot shall enforce geofencing by rejecting commands that lead the drone into unauthorized zones.
  • The Autopilot shall not accept climb commands beyond the predefined altitude ceiling.
  • The Autopilot shall initiate a controlled emergency landing if radio signal is lost for more than a specified timeout.
  • Upon detecting GPS signal degradation or spoofing, the Autopilot shall switch to inertial navigation and trigger Return-To-Home (RTH) if route deviation exceeds safe thresholds.
  • The Autopilot shall validate all telemetry data before transmission to the ground control to avoid misleading operator feedback.
  • he Autopilot shall perform periodic self-checks of critical sensors (IMU, GPS, Compass) and report anomalies.
  • The Autopilot shall discard commands received over radio if they are malformed or inconsistent with flight rules.
  • (R-04) Provide motor speed commands to ESC for thrust.
  • (R-05) Compute flight dynamics from sensor and mission data.
  • (R-06) Integrate position data into control logic.
  • (R-07) Send telemetry and sensor updates to operator.
  • (R-08) Exchange info with other components across a secure channel
